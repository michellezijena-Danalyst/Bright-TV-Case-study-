# Bright-TV-Case-study-
A case study on Bright tv viewership and analytics

BrightTV Growth Analysis: Understanding Viewers to Drive Subscriptions
The Context

BrightTV’s CEO set a clear goal for the year: grow the subscription base.

On the surface, that sounds straightforward. But behind that goal sits a more interesting question:
What actually makes people watch more — and come back?

That’s where this analysis comes in.
I was asked to work with BrightTV’s user and viewing data to uncover patterns in how people use the platform, what drives engagement, and where there are missed opportunities to increase both consumption and subscriptions.

The Problem Behind the Problem

Growing subscriptions isn’t just about acquiring new users. It’s about:

-Getting new users to actually watch something
-Giving existing users a reason to return
-Understanding when and why engagement drop

Without that, growth becomes expensive and unsustainable.

So instead of jumping straight to solutions, I started with the data to understand how people really behave on BrightTV.

The Data
The dataset included two main pieces:
-User profiles – who the users are
-Viewing sessions – what they watched, and when

Each row in the dataset represents a single viewing session, which made it possible to look closely at real user behavior over time.

One important step early on:
All timestamps were provided in UTC, so they were converted to South African time (SAST) to reflect actual viewing habits accurately.

How I Approached It

I treated this like a behavior problem, not just a reporting task.

Step 1: Make the data usable

Before any analysis, I:

-Cleaned inconsistencies
-Converted time zones
-Built features like:
Day of week
Time of day
Session duration
Viewing frequency per user

This made it easier to spot patterns that aren’t obvious at first glance.

Step 2: Understand how people actually watch

I explored questions like:

When are people most active?
Are weekends different from weekdays?
Do users binge, or watch in short bursts?
Peak watch times

This is where the first real insights started to show up.

Step 3: Identify what drives consumption

From the patterns, I looked at what influences engagement:

Time of day plays a big role there are clear peaks and quiet periods
Some users are highly active, while others barely engage
Session behavior varies some users stay longer, others dip in and out

This helped move from what is happening to why it might be happening.

Step 4: Spot the missed opportunities

One of the most interesting findings was the consistency of low-consumption periods.

These aren’t random — they happen at predictable times.

That creates an opportunity:
Instead of accepting low engagement, BrightTV can design for it.

For example:

Releasing lighter, more accessible content during quieter periods
Scheduling content drops when users are more likely to be idle
Using targeted recommendations to re-engage users at the right moment

Step 5: Think beyond content — think growth

Content alone doesn’t grow a platform.

Based on the patterns, I explored broader growth ideas:

Improving how new users are onboarded
Helping users find relevant content faster
Encouraging repeat usage through smarter recommendations
Using engagement patterns to guide marketing efforts

The goal is simple:
Make it easier for users to start watching and harder for them to stop.

What Stood Out

A few things became very clear:

Viewing behavior is strongly tied to time not all hours are equal
Engagement isn’t evenly distributed a smaller group of users drives a lot of activity
There are predictable gaps in consumption that can be strategically addressed
Retention and engagement are just as important as acquisition

Tools Used
SQL for data extraction and transformation
Excel for quick validation and exploration
Google slides

What This Project Shows

This project isn’t just about dashboards or charts.

It shows how raw viewing data can be turned into clear, practical decisions:

When to release content
How to engage users more effectively
Where growth opportunities actually exist
Final Thought

Subscription growth doesn’t come from one big idea.

It comes from understanding small patterns in user behavior and acting on them consistently.

That’s what this analysis aims to do.
turn everyday viewing data into smarter decisions that drive real growth.
